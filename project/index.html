Project

Working paper

Endogenous Network Formation in Local Public Goods: An Experimental Analysis (with Tom Lane and Stuart McDonald). CeDEx Discussion Paper Series ISSN 1749 - 3293. [Download Link]

Abstract: This paper experimentally explores the provision and consumption of public goods in networks characterized by heterogeneity in agents’ production costs and valuations of the good. We study how much agents produce and the network structures that are formed endogenously by their linking decisions which facilitate the sharing of output. Our experiment provides a direct test of the model of Kinateder & Merlino (2017), and corroborates its key insight of agents forming core-periphery networks, with a highly productive minority of agents who highly value the good or can produce it cheaply located at the core, connected to the majority of relatively unproductive agents who lie in the periphery. However, subjects often produce more and form denser networks than the model predicts , which sometimes reduces efficiency. There is some tendency for behaviour to converge towards the theoretical equilibrium as the game is played repeatedly. Our results help us understand the emergence of the ‘law of the few’ in real-world networks, and suggest it is driven by the endogenous sorting of heterogeneous agents.

Keywords: Local public goods; Network formation; Experiment; Heterogeneity
